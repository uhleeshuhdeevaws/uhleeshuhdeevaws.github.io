Hello! I'm Alicia DeVrio.

My research explores ways to more adequately remediate AI system harms through understanding, centering, and supporting the agency and resistance of those everyday people who use and are most impacted by algorithmic systems. I am on the job market for academic or industry research positions starting in 2026.

I’m a PhD candidate at Carnegie Mellon University’s Human-Computer Interaction Institute where I’m advised by Ken Holstein. Previously, I have conducted research on Microsoft Research Montreal’s fairness, accountability, transparency, and ethics (FATE) team with Su Lin Blodgett and Alexandra Olteanu, where I explored impacts of and interventions for anthropomorphic AI. My work has been published at top-tier computing venues including CHI, CSCW, FAccT, and ACL.

Before graduate school, I worked as a growth product manager at Noom and led digital product experimentation efforts for the Wall Street Journal and Barron’s at Dow Jones. I graduated from Brown University with an undergraduate degree in computer science and grew up in the Appalachian Mountains in Northeast Tennessee.

In my free time, I like to run, watch musicals, eat ice cream, and visit libraries. I also am a weaver who recently was an artist-in-residence at Praxis Fiber Workshop’s Digital Weaving Lab.
